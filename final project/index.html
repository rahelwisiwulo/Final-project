Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

Born: 1909 , Died: 1972
(in exile in Guinea) Profession: Teacher,
philosopher, politician
Legacy: Founder of the Convention
People's Party (CPP) in 1949. Led the
"Positive Action" campaign that
pressured the British to grant
independence. Became Ghana’s
first Prime Minister and later President.
Advocated for pan-Africanism,
industrialization, and education.
Overthrown in 1966; today
remembered as one of Africa’s
greatest leaders.

William Ofori Atta

Born: 1910, Died: 1988
Profession: Lawyer, educator, politician
Legacy: Known for his deep Christian
faith and moral leadership. Part of
UGCC and also helped form United Party
and People’s Movement for Freedom
and Justice later in life.
Believed in education and the
role of youth in development. Famous
for his humility and commitment to
ethics in politics.

Obetsebi-Lamptey

Born: 1902, Died: 1963
Profession: Lawyer, nationalist, organizer
Legacy: A brilliant orator
and organizer. Mobilized youth,
trade unions, and local leaders. Helped
shape the UGCC and its message
of independence. Father of
Jake Obetsebi-Lamptey (former Minister
and NPP Chairman).

Dr. J.B Danquah

Born: 1895
Died: 1965 (died in prison)
: Lawyer, scholar, politician
Legacy:Known as “The Doyen of Gold
Coast Politics.” Helped found the
United Gold Coast Convention (UGCC)
in 1947, the first political party
brin the Gold Coast.
Advocated for self-government through
constitutional reform.
He was a key figure in the early
nationalist movement in the Gold Coast.
He was a mentor to Kwame Nkrumah,
who later became Ghana’s first president.
He was a strong
believer in the rule of law and democracy.

He was a prolific writer and
intellectual, contributing significantly to
the political discourse of his time.
He gave Ghana its name, drawing from
the ancient Ghana Empire.

Ebenezer Ako-Adjei

Born: 1916, Died: 2002
Profession: Lawyer, politician
Legacy: Played a quiet but strategic role
in Ghana’s independence struggle.
He was the one who invited Kwame
Nkrumah to join the UGCC. Served as
Minister of Foreign Affairs under
Nkrumah. Known for his wisdom
and diplomacy.

Edward Akufo-Addo

Born: 1906, Died: 1979
Profession: Lawyer, judge, statesman
Legacy: Served as Chief Justice
(1966–1970) and later as President of
Ghana (1970–1972). Advocated for
legal reforms and justice during the
colonial era. A strong believer
in non-violence and constitutional
government. Father of current
President Nana Akufo-Addo.
